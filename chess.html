Chess


Pawn



  • ‒ Moves forward by one


  • ‒ Takes pieces ahead of it diagonally


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 8



    • Point Value: 1

    Knight



  • ‒ Moves and takes pieces in an L shape in any direction


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 2



    • Point Value: 3

    Bishop



  • ‒ Moves and takes pieces diagonally in any direction


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 2



    • Point Value: 3

    Rook



  • ‒ Moves forward, backwards, left, and right by any number of spaces


  • ‒ Takes pieces in the same directions


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 2



    • Point Value: 5

    King



  • ‒ Moves in all directions by one space


  • ‒ Takes pieces diagonally


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 1



    • No Point Value!

    Queen



  • ‒ Moves in all directions by any number of spaces


  • ‒ Takes pieces diagonally


  • ‒ Initial quantity: 1



    • Point Value: 9

    Special Moves:

    • Double Pawn Push - On its first move, a pawn can advance two squares instead of one.
    • En Passant - A pawn captures an opponent’s pawn that has been double pushed from its starting position.
    • Castling (O-O/O-O-O) - A move that puts the king in a defensive formation next to the rook.
    • Check (+) - A move that attacks the opponent’s king, requiring a move to protect it.
    • Pawn Promotion - A pawn on the 8th or 1st rank becomes a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
    • Checkmate (#) - The king can't move out of a check. Signifies the end of the game.
    • Stalemate - The player has no moves to make, resulting in a draw.
